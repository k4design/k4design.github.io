FAQs

Common first-timer questions

Answers to the things every new buyer wants to know.

You'll need money for a down payment (3%–20% depending on loan type), closing costs (2%–5% of the loan), and an emergency reserve. On a $400,000 home with 3.5% FHA down, plan for roughly $14,000–$30,000 out of pocket before moving in.
FHA loans typically require a 580+ score with 3.5% down (or 500 with 10% down). Conventional loans usually require 620+. A score above 740 earns you the best rates and can save thousands over the life of your loan.
From pre-approval to closing, most buyers take 4–6 months — including the time spent searching for the right home. Once your offer is accepted, closing itself typically takes 30–45 days for financing, inspections, and paperwork.
Technically no, but strongly recommended. A buyer's agent represents your interests, provides neighborhood expertise, handles negotiation, and is typically paid by the seller — so it costs you nothing. Going it alone on your first purchase is rarely worth the risk.
Closing costs are fees paid at settlement — typically 2%–5% of the loan amount. They cover loan origination, title insurance, appraisal, taxes, and more. Buyers usually pay them, but you can sometimes negotiate seller concessions to help offset the cost.
